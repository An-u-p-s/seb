CONSTITUTION OF INDIA
Background
1857 to 1947
Most of the Indian sub continent was under the British rule.
15th Aug 1947
The dominion of British India was divided into two independent dominions, based on religious issues:
1. The Dominion of India:
It became Republic of India on 26th January 1950.
2. The Dominion of Pakistan:
West Pakistan (Today's Pakistan) became Islamic Republic of Pakistan.
East Pakistan (Today's Bangladesh) became People's Republic of Bangladesh.
1947 to 1950
India was an independent independent dominion in the British CommonWealth of Nations. It continued same legislation for these three years. British Government is responsible for the external security of the country.
26th Jan 1950
The Constitution of India came into effective and replaced Government of India Act, 1935 as the country's fundamental governing document and the Dominion of India became Republic of India.
Evolution of Indian Constitution
The idea of making Indian Constitution was happened before 1947, it was evolved from the demands of the people expressed in Indian National Movements and from the people in response to the British acts before Independence.

It was first proposed by M.N.Roy in 1934 and later, it became the official demand of Indian National Congress (INC) in 1935.
